What Are Biopotentials?

Every cell in your body produces tiny electrical signals called biopotentials. Your body is literally a biological battery.

🧠

EEG

Brain waves · 0.5-100 Hz · ~10 uV

💪

EMG

Muscle signals · 20-500 Hz · 0.1-10 mV

❤️

ECG

Heart electrical · 0.05-150 Hz · 1-5 mV

Analogy: Just like your phone sends data wirelessly, your brain sends electrical signals to your muscles to move them.

What is an ADC?

Biosignals are analog (continuous waves). Computers only understand digital (0s and 1s). The ADC translates between them.

TypeBitsMax ValueDevice
8-bit8255Old microcontrollers
10-bit101023Arduino Uno/Nano
12-bit124095ESP32, STM32
24-bit2416M+Medical ECG machines
Analogy: An ADC is like a translator between your body and the computer. More bits = more detail, like higher camera megapixels.

EMG — Your Muscles Have a Voice

Electromyography measures electrical signals produced by muscles when they contract. Electrodes are placed on the skin surface above the muscle.

ECG — Your Heart's Electrical Story

Electrocardiography records the electrical activity of the heart. Each heartbeat produces a characteristic PQRST waveform.

WaveWhat Happens
PAtria contract (top chambers pump)
QRSVentricles contract (the main big beat)
TVentricles reset (repolarize)

Coding Block — Arduino Biosignal Reader

Goal: Read a biosignal from sensor pin A0, convert to voltage, print to Serial Monitor.

// Biosignal Reader - Arduino Uno (10-bit ADC)
// Tools → Serial Plotter to see live graph!

void setup() {
  Serial.begin(9600);
}

void loop() {
  int   rawValue = analogRead(A0);
  float voltage  = (rawValue / 1023.0) * 5.0;
  Serial.print("Raw: ");   Serial.print(rawValue);
  Serial.print("  V: ");   Serial.println(voltage);
  delay(10);
}
ESP32 users: Change 1023.0 to 4095.0 and baud to 115200
